Below on this page are some songs that we are going to sing and play on guitar together. These are not technically challenging songs, as the chords and strumming patterns can be learned quickly, even if you've never played guitar.

The richness of these songs rather comes from your feeling as you sing and play them, and that is a richness that will continue to grow over a lifetime.

The point isn't to learn to play guitar. The point of these songs is to learn the language of music. Music can have different feelings, including funny, just like spoken language.

If you've never played and sung, don't think that you can't do it. Everyone is capable of singing and playing music. Can't all birds of the same species learn to make music with similar capacity? Then why can't all humans of the same species learn to make music with similar capacity?

Humans aren't born with birth given gifts, rather abilities are developed over a lifetime. Now it's true that you may not have had the environment to learn music growing up, but your inability to speak the language of sound is not due to a lacking of your human capability, only a lacking of your surroundings.

It's not hard or easy to make music. It just takes time and the proper environment to grow your confidence. It's like riding a bike. You get more confident the more you do it.

If you don't have a guitar, any one will be enough to get going, preferably one that is an acoustic-electric so that you can eventually plug it in with a mic to record yourself playing and singing.

The first time you hear yourself will be bizarre. Don't think you suck, just understand you have growing room. Everyone starts at the beginning.

If you want to wait to purchase equipment like a guitar and mic, we can place an order together to get a volume discount. Eventually we'll be making guitars and mics within this organization, but for now we'll need something off-the-shelf to get learning.


--------------
If you're looking to get going right away, here's the equipment I use:
  • For guitar I use a Martin Sigma DM-1STCE that I purchased for about $250 about 20 years ago. You don't need to go out and spend 1000 bucks on a guitar, especially if you've never played. The sound of the music you create is more about your playing than the guitar's construction. I did a quick look on AMZN and purchased this Ashthorpe Full-Size Cutaway Thinline for 125 bucks to see how it plays. Most reviews say that they are surprised by how well it plays. And let me tell you, after only one day of having it I already enjoy playing it. Some people have had issues with the thing breaking, so something to keep in mind, but it seems that the majority of players love this guitar. It comes with everything out of the box. If you want to try it out before you buy it, I'll bring this guitar out to meetings for you to check out. I got the natural color and it's beautiful to look at.
  • For a microphone I use an MXL Mics 770 Cardioid Condenser Microphone. You can spend way more, but this one sounds lovely.
  • For audio input I use a Behringer UMC404HD, which I purchased for 100 bucks back in 2018. You'll need something like this to record into a computer. It's out of stock quite often and I've seen the price as high as $200 from AMZN. Other sellers have been selling it for higher. You could get away with one that has 2 inputs, but I like the four inputs in case I want to go beyond just plugging in a guitar and a microphone. And the build quality of the UMC404HD is fantastic.
  • In terms of which application to use for recording audio only without video, I use AUDACITY, which is free and opensource. In Audacity you can record all 4 channels of the Behringer UMC404HD at the same time. It's super high quality, so you get recordings that can be hundreds of MB for only minutes of play. And you can compress them down in Audacity to a format like OGG, which is similar to MP3, but opensource.
  • For recording audio with video I use QuickTime on a MacBook. The nice thing about QuickTime is if you use a recording device like the Behringer with two inputs coming in, one for the guitar and one for a mic, QuickTime will automatically put one on the left ear and the other on the right ear, so you can later adjust the guitar and mic separately in something like Audacity or GarageBand.

-------------- My mother used to sing to me as a baby. If your mom or dad or sister or brother did that for you, then you'll be able to pick up these songs quick. For those HUMANS that didn't have someone hum and rock them as a baby, we'll have to figure out how to rekindle your music bone. It'll involve building your confidence to sing and move your body with the music. It's about finding flow. A loose way of feeling music, and not a metronome type of playing. Most days of playing and singing are incremental. You'll progress bit by bit on those days. But every now and then you get a day where you make a leap. You'll surprise yourself. Something that you never thought you would be able to do, you can now do with confidence. So keep playing, and one day will come when you make a leap. Over time as you grow your understanding of music, you just sort of get better automatically. It's not like you drill playing these songs. It's more like you work on life and get more confident in everything in life, and then every now and then you notice that your singing gets better and better as your confidence in being a HUMAN grows. One last note, you don't need to play for hours every day. Play when you feel like it. Playing for minutes in a day is enough to grow your musical abilities over the years.
