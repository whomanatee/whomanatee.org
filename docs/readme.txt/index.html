This is a guide on navigating the ideas behind this organization. Here are some places to start looking around:


------------------------------------
A) If you haven't already done so, you can sign-up for a family meeting near you that will discuss everything on this site.


------------------------------------
B) The first sets of meetings will discuss the words on onewordmatters.org. A family 🔥BBQ🔥 will follow all family meetings.


------------------------------------
C) The family business proposal contains a high level plan of the family business.


------------------------------------
D) Take a look at the books in the library:
  • this is a reference library and not a list of books to finish ASAP
  • you don't need to read every single page of every book
  • all main ideas will be discussed in family meetings
  • owning any one of these books is enough to get going
  • go at your own pace, sometimes reading one sentence is enough to keep the mind occupied for days
  • sometimes it's useful to think about the name of the book, as in why did the author reduce the book to that title
  • peruse the table of contents to get an idea of what the author is trying to express
  • peruse quotes that are sometimes put at the beginning of chapters
  • peruse appendices
  • peruse introductions
  • peruse first chapters
  • sometimes it makes sense to skip ahead and peruse the last chapter
  • peruse any other chapters that catch your eye
  • some books also put summaries at the end of chapters, which are useful even before reading the chapter
  • AMZN often has previews of books that are enough to begin pondering the topics covered in the book

------------------------------------
E) Hone your abilities for public persuasion.

The book THANK YOU FOR ARGUING is a fantastic place to start.

A useful way of understanding the title would be to think of it as THANK YOU FOR ARGUING THE TRUTH, as Jay isn't saying to argue just to argue, but to argue for the betterment of society.

And bettering society comes from THE TRUTH, and the truth is that we were all born with equal LIFE, equal LIBERTY, and equal capacity for CHOICE.

Usually when we think of arguing, we think of it as being something that we don't want to do. But Jay is saying no, no, thank you for taking the time to argue with me to better the world.

Jay changed my life, I'll never forget the moment I first saw his book. Jay says similar about the book that got him thinking about the ideas of public persuasion, which was written by John Quincy Adams. And that's a book in the library that I have not read one page of as of yet, but it's on my kitchen counter soon to be perused. And here's a digital copy of volume 1 of John's book.

And here's a book by Aristotle whose first few pages are enough to get you going and excited. A valuable discovery that Aristotle had was he said that if you are after the truth, take people in front of a public audience to expose lies and get the truth. People's true nature is exposed in front of an audience, and the audience will be able to see who is being honest and who is lying.

To master persuasion requires that one master the detection of lies. This is because people are not always honest and will claim things that they know are not true. It takes years to get better at detecting lies and there are multiple books in the library that deal with finding the truth.

In conclusion, stand for only truth and you'll never need to be nervous to argue in front of the human public.

No need for anger, no need for bullying, only a need for honesty.

Easy peasy.
