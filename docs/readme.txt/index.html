This is a guide on navigating the ideas behind this organization. Here are some places to start looking around:

--------------
A) The site MAGAY.org has many details about plans for this organization.

--------------
B) If you haven't already done so, you can sign-up for a meeting.

--------------
C) On many pages on this site are lists of words that can be clicked. When looking through the words, the point isn't to memorize every term, but to understand the meaning and origins behind every term that you do choose to read about. Listed below are the first sets of topics that will be discussed at meetings.
  • to PERSUADE or be PERSUADED
  • EXISTENCE
  • WORDS
  • GROUPS
  • ETHNICS
--------------
D) Take a look at the books in the library:
  • you don't need to read every single page ASAP
  • go at your own pace, sometimes reading one sentence is enough to keep the mind occupied for days
  • all main ideas will be discussed in meetings
  • it's useful to think about the name of the book, as in why did the author reduce the book to that title
  • look at the table of contents to get an idea of what the author is trying to express
  • peruse quotes that are sometimes put at the beginnings of chapters
  • peruse appendices
  • peruse introductions
  • peruse first chapters
  • sometimes it makes sense to skip ahead and peruse the last chapter
  • peruse any other chapters that catch your eye
  • some books also put summaries at the end of chapters, which are useful even before reading the chapter
  • AMZN often has previews of books that are enough to begin pondering the topics covered in the book
--------------
E) Hone your abilities for public persuasion. The book THANK YOU FOR ARGUING is a fantastic place to start.

A useful way of understanding the title would be to think of it as THANK YOU FOR ARGUING THE TRUTH, as Jay isn't saying to argue just to argue, but to argue for the betterment of society.

And bettering society comes from THE TRUTH, and the truth is that we were all born with equal LIFE, equal LIBERTY, and equal capacity for CHOICE.

Usually when we think of arguing, we think of it as being something that we don't want to do. But Jay is saying no, no, thank you for taking the time to argue with me to better the world.

Jay changed my life, I'll never forget the moment I first saw his book. Jay says similar about the book that got him thinking about the ideas of public oratory, which was written by John Quincy Adams. And that's a book in the library that I have not read one page of as of yet, but it's on my kitchen counter soon to be perused. And here's a digital copy of volume 1 of John's book available online.

And here's a book by Aristotle, which I've as of yet only read the first few pages of, which were enough to get me going and excited on his thinking. One thing he said is to take people in front of a public audience to show their true nature, for both the truth and lies will be exposed.

One thing to master is being able to detect lies, because people are not honest and will argue things that are not true. This is why there are multiple books in the library that deal with lying.

Stand for truth and you'll never need to be nervous to argue in front of the human public.

No need for anger, no need for bullying, only a need for honesty. Easy peasy.
